Change 1: Section 33 Categorisation

Correction S33 must be explicitly categorised into S33(2) and S33(3) with distinct letter types and assessment tracks

Source: John Malungani email point 1; “Application of Legal Principles” presentation slides 12–18

Rationale

Section 33 of the NWA has two distinct regimes that were previously treated as a single category. S33(2) is the Kader Asmal Declaration for scheduled areas under irrigation board schemes — dormant but paid-for volumes count as ELU. S33(3) is a declaration on individual application with two sub-categories (a) and (b). Each has distinct letter types, assessment tracks, and workflow paths. The system must distinguish between these to generate the correct letters and apply the correct assessment logic.

BEFORE

  • Section 33 treated as a single undifferentiated provision
  • No S33 letter types defined
  • No AssessmentTrack field on FileMaster
  • Single workflow track for all cases after CP9
  • No irrigation board / dormant volume tracking on LetterIssuance

AFTER

  • S33(2) = Kader Asmal Declaration (scheduled areas, irrigation boards, dormant volumes = ELU)
  • S33(3)(a) and S33(3)(b) = Declaration on individual application (two sub-types)
  • 3 new letter types: S33(2) Declaration, S33(3)(a) Declaration, S33(3)(b) Declaration
  • FileMaster.AssessmentTrack field: "S35_Verification" | "S33_2_Declaration" | "S33_3_Declaration"
  • Post-CP9 workflow branches into Track A (S35), Track B (S33(2)), or Track C (S33(3))
  • LetterIssuance.IrrigationBoardId FK and IncludesDormantVolume flag for S33(2) cases
  • 2 new workflow states: S33_2_DeclarationIssued, S33_3_DeclarationIssued

Change 2: Qualifying Period Unified

Correction Single qualifying period for both surface water and ground water

Source: John Malungani email point 2; SFRA court order direction

Rationale

The qualifying period was previously modelled as two separate periods: one for surface water and one for borehole/ground water. Per the SFRA court order, there is a single unified qualifying period of 1 October 1996 – 30 September 1998 for all water types. There is no legal basis for distinguishing between surface and borehole qualifying dates.

BEFORE

  • Two separate qualifying periods in seed data:
    • Surface water: 1 Oct 1996 – 30 Sep 1998
    • Borehole/ground water: 1 Oct 1997 – 30 Sep 1999
  • Wireframes displayed split dates
  • LEGAL-FRAMEWORK.md had separate rows per water type

AFTER

  • Single qualifying period: 1 October 1996 – 30 September 1998
  • Applies to all water types (surface, ground, SFRA)
  • Single Period seed record: “Qualifying Period: 1 Oct 1996 - 30 Sep 1998”
  • All documentation, wireframes, and diagrams updated to reflect single period
Affected artefacts: Services/SeedDataService.cs (Period seed data), docs/LEGAL-FRAMEWORK.md (lines 185–186), docs/Data-Structures-Document.html, docs/Workflow-Document.html, docs/wireframes/modern-internal-wireframes.html, CLAUDE.md §Qualifying Period, 04-data-flow-per-property.mmd [AMENDED], 14-end-to-end-data-flow.mmd [AMENDED]

Change 3: Two-Tier Legal Framework (GWCA Proclamation Rules)

Additive Configurable per-GWCA legal limits that override general principles

Source: John Malungani “Application of Legal Principles” presentation slides 22–38; email point 3

Rationale

The lawfulness assessment operates at two levels. General Legal Principles (riparian rights, S9B limits, private water) apply to all properties outside proclaimed areas. Specific Legal Principles are per-GWCA proclamation rules published in the Government Gazette that override general principles for properties within that area. Each proclaimed GWCA has unique limits (e.g. max hectares, max volume per hectare, max storage). These must be modelled as configurable rules, not hardcoded values.

BEFORE

  • No explicit two-tier framework in the data model
  • GovernmentWaterControlArea entity existed but had no proclamation rules
  • No gazette reference or proclamation date fields
  • Lawfulness assessment logic had no GWCA rule lookup

AFTER

  • New entity: GwcaProclamationRule — configurable per-GWCA legal limits
  • GovernmentWaterControlArea enhanced with GovernmentGazetteReference, ProclamationDate, and ProclamationRules collection
  • LawfulnessAssessmentService design: checks GWCA rules first, then falls through to general principles
  • Blyde River GWCA example rules seeded (5 rules from GN 180 and GN 2805)

Change 4: Three-Phase Process (Labels Corrected)

Correction Validation and Verification phase labels were swapped in the previous design

Source: John Malungani email point 4; presentation slides 5–10

Rationale

The V&V process has three phases, not two, and the labels were previously reversed in the codebase. Validation is the technical phase (did the water use exist, and to what extent?) and Verification is the legal phase (was the water use lawful?). The previous design had these labels swapped in workflow states, seed data, diagrams, and documentation.

BEFORE

  • Two phases only (no explicit Inception phase)
  • Phase labels swapped:
    • CP2–CP6 were labelled “Verification” (wrong — should be Validation)
    • CP7–CP9 were labelled “Validation” (wrong — should be Verification)
  • Workflow states had Phase = "Phase1", "Phase2"
  • Diagrams showed “Phase 2 (Implementation)”

AFTER

  • Three phases:
    1. Inception (CP1) — project setup, data acquisition, public participation
    2. Validation (CP2–CP6) — Technical: determine if water use EXISTED and ESTIMATE EXTENT
    3. Verification (CP7–CP9 + letters) — Legal: determine if water use was LAWFUL
  • Workflow states use phase names: Phase = "Inception", "Validation", "Verification"
  • Key distinction: Validation = “Did the use exist and how much?” (GIS-based). Verification = “Was it lawful?” (instrument-based)

Change 5: Mapbooks (Processed GIS Map Products)

Additive V&V file artefact is a processed Mapbook, not raw satellite images

Source: John Malungani email point 5

Rationale

The V&V file (Appendix A, items 7 and 9) contains a processed Mapbook — a GIS map product with references to the source satellite images used to produce it. Raw satellite images (SateliteImage records) are uploaded into the system separately and are not directly part of the V&V file. The workflow guard at CP5 must check for the presence of Mapbook records, not raw SateliteImage records.

BEFORE

  • V&V file referenced raw satellite images directly
  • No Mapbook or MapbookImage entities
  • CP5 guard would check for SateliteImage presence
  • Appendix A items 7 & 9 described as “satellite imagery”

AFTER

  • New entity: Mapbook — processed GIS map product per FileMaster
  • New entity: MapbookImage — join table linking Mapbook to source SateliteImage records
  • CP5 guard checks for Mapbook presence (qualifying + current periods)
  • Appendix A items 7 & 9 now “Qualifying/Current period Mapbook”
  • Raw SateliteImage records remain as a separate upload mechanism

Change 6: CatchmentArea in Organisational Hierarchy

Additive Quaternary catchment as an explicit entity between WMA and Property

Source: Edwin Matlapeng / DWS feedback on organisational hierarchy; presentation slide 4 (catchment hierarchy)

Rationale

The organisational hierarchy must explicitly include Catchment Area (quaternary catchment, e.g. “A21A”, “B31A”) as a level between Water Management Area and Property. Previously, catchment data was stored as free-text string fields (Property.QuaternaryDrainage, FileMaster.QuaternaryCatchment) with no referential integrity. The new CatchmentArea entity provides a proper FK relationship and enables scoping of cases by catchment for access control.

BEFORE

  • Hierarchy: National → Provincial → Regional/WMA → CMA → Property
  • Catchment stored as free-text: Property.QuaternaryDrainage (string)
  • Catchment stored as free-text: FileMaster.QuaternaryCatchment (string)
  • OrganisationalUnit.Type values: National, Provincial, Regional, CMA
  • No referential integrity on catchment data

AFTER

  • Hierarchy: National → Provincial → Regional/WMA → Catchment Area → CMA → Property
  • New entity: CatchmentArea (Guid PK, CatchmentCode unique index, FK to WaterManagementArea)
  • Property.CatchmentAreaId FK (nullable, legacy string field retained for migration)
  • FileMaster.CatchmentAreaId FK (nullable, legacy string field retained for migration)
  • OrganisationalUnit.CatchmentAreaId FK + "Catchment" added to Type values
  • ~1,946 quaternary catchments nationally (e.g. “A21A”, “B31A”)
